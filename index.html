My Journey

In February 2019, I quit my job.

Why did I quit? Let me tell you about it. I was a personal trainer at a gym in Uptown called Dynamic Fitness, and at first, I loved it. Sure, I was living with my dad in a small apartment, but I had just moved to Houston from a small town in Mississippi. I was able to spend all day in the gym, work in gym clothes (SO comfortable), and met many interesting people. During my year and a half at Dynamic Fitness, I realized I was stuck. I had no career growth, nor any potential income increase in sight. Meanwhile, my friends were getting promoted and Christmas bonuses. Soon I was thinking, “Wait! I wanted a Christmas bonus too!”. But I kept at it. Just kept the grind.

Was I happy?

That’s the question of the hour right there. The more I thought about that question the more I realized that I wasn’t happy with where my life was at the time. I was comfortably stuck in a low-paying job with no end in sight.

A Man Name Rahul

Surprisingly, change first started from a client named Rahul. At first, he was just any other client I trained 3 times a week. Let me tell you, this guy was a sponge. All information I gave him about exercise, he absorbed it. But what makes Rahul a key part of my story here was during our normal Wednesday morning training session. He was a Software Engineer for some marketing company in Houston, and he LOVED his job. He would constantly talk about solving stack issues or his latest data structure assignment. One day though, in the middle of him doing cable fly’s, he looked at me and asked one important question. Where do you see yourself in 5 years? I made some joke to try to move the question along, but he wasn’t having it. That’s when he suggested to go back to school do what he did. “Go back to school, Jordan”. “Become a Software Engineer, Jordan”. He kept pushing it and pushing it until I finally caved and did some research.

Change

Not even three weeks later, I’m enrolled in The University of Houston’s Computer Science program. Not even joking, it was less than a month that I was suddenly a college student again waiting for classes to start. Two and a half years later, I’ve made apps that I could have only dreamed about making. I’ve learned about those “mysterious computer languages” that never made sense to me before. I’m applying for jobs that pay more than double, sometimes triple, what I was earning at Dynamic Fitness. More important than all of that…I’m optimistic for the future.
